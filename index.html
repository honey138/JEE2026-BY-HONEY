JEE 2026: Joint Entrance Examination Information Portal

About JEE 2026

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is India’s premier engineering entrance exam, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to top institutes like IITs, NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs.

  • Exam Dates: Session 1 (Jan 2026), Session 2 (April 2026)
  • Eligibility: Passed/appearing 12th (2024, 2025, 2026), no age limit
  • Attempts: 2 per year, max 6 in 3 years
  • Languages: 13 regional languages

Syllabus & Exam Pattern

  • Mathematics: Algebra, Calculus, Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, Probability, Statistics, Vectors, 3D Geometry
  • Physics: Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Waves, Optics, Electricity & Magnetism, Modern Physics
  • Chemistry: Physical, Organic, Inorganic, Environmental Chemistry
  • Pattern: Computer-based, 3 sections (Math, Physics, Chemistry), MCQs & numerical questions

Preparation Tips

  • Follow a daily study schedule and revise regularly
  • Practice mock tests and previous year papers
  • Focus on weak areas and analyze mistakes
  • Take breaks, eat healthy, and get enough sleep
  • Stay positive and avoid last-minute cramming

Cut-off & Results

  • Expected Cut-off (General): ~90 percentile
  • Result: Scorecard with percentile, rank, and cut-off; top scorers eligible for JEE Advanced

Counselling & Admission

  • JoSAA counselling for seat allocation in IITs, NITs, IIITs, GFTIs
  • Participating institutes: 100+ across India
